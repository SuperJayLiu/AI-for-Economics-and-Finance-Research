# When Not To Use AI

Do not use AI when the task requires confidentiality, authorization, or expert judgment that you cannot verify.

Avoid AI for:

- uploading restricted data to public tools
- creating or altering empirical results
- inventing robustness checks
- writing referee reports against policy
- fabricating citations or literature positioning
- deciding identification strategy without expert review
- producing investment advice from research findings
- replacing required reading of primary sources

If the verification burden is larger than the benefit, do not use AI for that task.
