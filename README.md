# AI for Economics and Finance Scholars

A practical GitHub-native guide to using AI and large language models responsibly in economics and finance research, from basic skills to advanced research automation.

## What This Repository Is

This handbook helps economics and finance scholars use AI across the research workflow without treating AI output as evidence. It focuses on skills, workflows, project setups, verification practices, and research accountability.

It is designed for:

- PhD students and research assistants
- Junior faculty and applied economists
- Finance researchers and policy researchers
- Instructors who want to teach responsible AI use in research

## What AI Can Help With

AI can be useful for:

- organizing literature notes
- clarifying research questions and mechanisms
- explaining code and statistical workflows
- reviewing Stata, R, Python, MATLAB, and LaTeX drafts
- improving paper structure and language
- preparing seminar talks and referee responses
- building repeatable research workflows

## What AI Should Not Be Trusted With

AI should not be treated as:

- a citation database
- proof of a causal claim
- a substitute for reading original papers
- a referee for confidential work unless policy permits use
- a safe place for restricted, proprietary, or confidential data
- an authority on algebra, proofs, empirical design, or institutional detail without manual checking

## Core Principle

Use AI to improve research process, not to replace research judgment.

For every AI-assisted output, ask:

- What did the AI use as input?
- What could it have invented or misunderstood?
- What must be verified in the original source, code, data, or derivation?
- What changes did the human researcher accept?
- Is any disclosure, privacy review, or version-control record needed?
