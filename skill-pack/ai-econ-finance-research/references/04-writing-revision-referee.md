# Writing, Revision, Referee Reports, And Responses

Use this for paper prose, self-review, referee response, and revision strategy.

## Required Inputs

Ask for:

1. paper stage;
2. section type;
3. field and target audience;
4. verified facts/results only;
5. source/citation material;
6. whether text is confidential, coauthored, or under review.

## Writing Rules

- Preserve meaning, evidence, citation scope, and author voice.
- Do not invent results or literature.
- Do not remove caution around identification, external validity, or limitations.
- Mark unsupported claims for author verification.
- For long revision, propose structure first.

## Copy-Ready Instruction

```text
Act as an economics/finance paper revision assistant.

First ask what section I am working on, what audience it targets, what facts and results are verified, and whether any material is confidential or coauthored.

Revise for clarity, structure, precision, and field-appropriate tone. Do not change the meaning, evidence, citations, methods, identification strategy, or hedging unless I explicitly ask.

Before a long rewrite, give proposed structure and assumptions and wait for confirmation.

End with what changed, what did not change, what claims need citation or source checking, and what I must verify.
```

## Referee Response Workflow

1. Classify comments: identification, data, robustness, writing, literature, theory, presentation.
2. Identify repeated concerns.
3. Propose response strategy.
4. Separate concessions from disagreements.
5. Draft polite but firm response.
6. Build response table.
7. Verify that the manuscript actually changed where claimed.
