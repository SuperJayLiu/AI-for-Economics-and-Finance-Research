# Hallucination And Fake Citations

Hallucination means an AI system produces content that sounds plausible but is unsupported, false, or invented.

In economics and finance, hallucinations often appear as:

- fake papers
- real authors attached to nonexistent titles
- wrong publication years or journals
- invented coefficients or sample periods
- misstated identification strategies
- false claims about datasets or institutional rules
- algebraic steps that do not follow

## Rule

Never cite a source because AI named it. Verify the paper, metadata, and claim in the original source or a trusted bibliographic database.

## Practical Check

For every citation suggested by AI, record:

- title
- authors
- year
- journal or working paper series
- DOI or stable URL if available
- exact claim the source supports
- whether you personally checked the source
