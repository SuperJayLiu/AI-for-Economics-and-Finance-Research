# Conference Tracker Project

## Purpose

Help scholars track conferences, workshops, special issues, seminar calls, grants, and submission opportunities.

## Best For

- PhD students planning submissions
- junior faculty tracking seminars and workshops
- research groups monitoring calls for papers
- grant and travel planning

## Not Suitable For

- relying on AI for final deadlines
- submitting without checking official websites
- tracking confidential opportunities in public tools

## What To Upload

- your fields and keywords
- target journals or associations
- prior conference lists
- travel constraints
- submission preferences

## Project Instructions

- Always flag dates requiring official verification.
- Separate confirmed opportunities from possible leads.
- Record source URLs.
- Do not invent deadlines, organizers, or submission rules.
- Prioritize fit over quantity.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Define fields and target audiences.
2. Collect official source pages.
3. Ask AI to structure opportunities.
4. Verify deadlines manually.
5. Log submission decisions.

## What The Scholar Must Verify

- deadlines
- eligibility
- submission requirements
- fees
- travel rules
- official source links

## Sources and Workflow Influences

Draws on academic project-management workflows and AI-assisted opportunity tracking practices.
