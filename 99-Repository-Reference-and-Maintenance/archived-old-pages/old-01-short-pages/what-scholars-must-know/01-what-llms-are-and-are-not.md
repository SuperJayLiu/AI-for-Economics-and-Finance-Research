# What LLMs Are And Are Not

Large language models predict and generate text, code, and structured outputs from patterns learned during training and from context provided during a session.

They are useful for:

- explanation
- organization
- drafting
- critique
- coding assistance
- translation between technical and nontechnical language

They are not:

- citation databases
- guaranteed search engines
- proof assistants
- econometricians
- referees
- replacements for reading sources, running code, or checking derivations

For research, treat an LLM as a skilled but unreliable assistant. It can accelerate work, but it cannot carry scholarly responsibility.
