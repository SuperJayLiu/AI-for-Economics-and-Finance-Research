# Responsible Use And Risks

Responsible AI use in research starts with accountability. The scholar remains responsible for claims, citations, analysis, code, interpretation, and disclosure.

> [!WARNING]
> The most dangerous AI output is not obviously wrong. It is fluent, plausible, and slightly false.

This section covers:

- human accountability
- AI disclosure
- privacy and unpublished work
- confidential data and referee reports
- fake citations
- bias in economic and financial contexts
- copyright and licenses
- when not to use AI
- data safety by data type
- submission checks

## Read This Before Uploading Anything

| Material | First question |
| --- | --- |
| data | Is the license or access agreement compatible with this tool? |
| coauthor draft | Did coauthors agree? |
| referee material | Does the journal or conference permit AI use? |
| code | Can I inspect and revert changes? |
| paper text | Will AI preserve citations, hedging, and meaning? |
