# Theory Paper Project

## Purpose

Support theory work by clarifying assumptions, intuition, notation, proposition statements, and proof structure.

## Best For

- explaining a model
- finding ambiguous assumptions
- preparing intuition
- checking exposition
- identifying missing cases
- drafting proof outlines

## Not Suitable For

- accepting unverified algebra
- proving results automatically
- deciding equilibrium conditions without checking
- making welfare or uniqueness claims without derivation

## What To Upload

- model setup
- assumptions
- notation
- propositions
- proof drafts
- related theoretical mechanisms

## What Not To Upload

- confidential working papers without permission
- coauthor material without agreement
- private seminar feedback if sensitive

## Project Instructions

- Preserve notation exactly.
- Separate formal derivation from intuition.
- Flag every step that requires manual checking.
- Identify missing cases or boundary conditions.
- Do not assert equilibrium, uniqueness, comparative statics, or welfare results without derivation.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Ask AI to restate assumptions and notation.
2. Ask for a plain-language mechanism.
3. Ask for possible missing cases.
4. Review proposition statements for clarity.
5. Use AI to produce proof-check questions.
6. Check every mathematical step manually.
7. Use AI for exposition after formal logic is verified.

## What The AI Should Produce

- notation summaries
- assumption checklists
- intuition drafts
- proof-outline comments
- boundary-case questions

## What The Scholar Must Verify

- algebra
- logic
- existence and uniqueness arguments
- comparative statics
- welfare claims
- consistency of notation

## Common Mistakes

- treating fluent proof text as a proof
- ignoring boundary cases
- accepting changed notation
- using AI to hide unclear assumptions

## Disclosure Notes

Record whether AI assisted with exposition, proof checking, or notation cleanup.
