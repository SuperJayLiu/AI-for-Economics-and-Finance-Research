# Revision Response Project

## Purpose

Help organize referee comments, plan revisions, and draft response language.

## Best For

- classifying comments
- detecting repeated concerns
- creating a revision plan
- drafting polite response language
- tracking paper changes

## Not Suitable For

- deciding what to concede without author judgment
- fabricating analyses
- uploading confidential reports when policy forbids it
- writing responses that misrepresent changes

## What To Upload

- referee letters if policy permits
- editor letter if policy permits
- manuscript sections
- revision notes
- completed tables or robustness results

## What Not To Upload

- confidential materials unless journal policy and tool privacy settings permit use
- unpublished coauthor material without consent
- data or results that cannot be shared

## Project Instructions

- Classify comments as major, minor, clarification, robustness, writing, or scope.
- Identify repeated concerns.
- Draft responses that are respectful, specific, and accurate.
- Do not invent new analyses or results.
- Flag places where the authors must decide whether to concede or push back.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Confirm AI use is allowed for the review materials.
2. Summarize comments by theme.
3. Create a revision plan.
4. Track each manuscript change.
5. Draft response language only after actual changes are made.
6. Verify that every response matches the manuscript.

## What The AI Should Produce

- comment taxonomy
- revision checklist
- response-letter outline
- draft response paragraphs
- consistency checks between response and manuscript

## What The Scholar Must Verify

- confidentiality rules
- accuracy of comment interpretation
- completed analyses
- manuscript changes
- tone and strategic choices

## Common Mistakes

- uploading referee material without checking policy
- drafting responses before doing revisions
- conceding too much or too little based on AI advice
- claiming changes that were not made

## Disclosure Notes

Follow journal policy. Keep an internal record of AI assistance and human verification.
