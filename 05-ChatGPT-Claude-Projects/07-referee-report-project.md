# Referee Report Project

## Purpose

Help structure a referee report when AI use is allowed by the journal, conference, or reviewing body.

## Best For

- organizing major and minor comments
- checking whether criticism is clearly stated
- separating contribution, identification, theory, and exposition issues
- improving tone

## Not Suitable For

- uploading confidential manuscripts when policy does not permit it
- replacing the referee's judgment
- generating criticism without reading the paper
- inventing robustness requests

## What To Upload

- manuscript only if policy permits
- your own nonconfidential notes
- journal review criteria
- your draft report

## What Not To Upload

- confidential material unless explicitly allowed
- editor correspondence
- private author information
- data or appendices subject to access restrictions

## Project Instructions

- Treat the human referee as fully responsible.
- Separate summary, contribution, major concerns, minor concerns, and recommendation logic.
- Do not invent claims about the paper.
- Flag comments that require direct textual evidence.
- Keep tone professional and specific.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Check the journal or conference AI policy.
2. Read the manuscript yourself.
3. Draft your own main assessment.
4. Use AI to organize and clarify the report only if permitted.
5. Verify every criticism against the manuscript.
6. Remove unsupported or excessive claims.

## What The AI Should Produce

- report outline
- comment classification
- tone edits
- clarity checks
- list of comments requiring evidence

## What The Scholar Must Verify

- permission to use AI
- all claims about the manuscript
- fairness of criticism
- confidentiality rules
- final recommendation logic

## Common Mistakes

- using AI before reading the paper
- uploading confidential material without permission
- letting AI create generic objections
- overstating identification flaws without evidence

## Disclosure Notes

Follow the review policy. If AI use is disallowed or unclear, do not upload the manuscript or review materials.
