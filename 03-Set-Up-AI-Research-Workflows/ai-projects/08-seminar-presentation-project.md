# Seminar Presentation Project

## Purpose

Help turn a research paper or project into a clear economics or finance seminar talk.

## Best For

- job market talks
- department seminars
- conference presentations
- PhD workshops
- internal research updates

## Not Suitable For

- hiding weak identification
- overstating results
- producing slides without understanding the audience
- replacing practice and expert feedback

## What To Upload

- paper draft or extended abstract
- target audience
- time limit
- key tables or figures
- presentation constraints
- likely audience concerns

## What Not To Upload

- confidential drafts without permission
- restricted data outputs
- private referee or seminar comments unless allowed

## Project Instructions

- Identify the economic question early.
- Keep the mechanism and identification clear.
- Do not overstate causality or policy relevance.
- Separate main results from robustness.
- Generate likely questions and concise answers.
- Flag slides where evidence may be too thin.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Define audience and time.
2. Extract the core research story.
3. Create a talk outline.
4. Decide which tables and figures belong in the main talk.
5. Prepare backup-slide topics.
6. Generate likely Q&A.
7. Check every claim against the paper.
8. Practice and revise.

## What The AI Should Produce

- talk outline
- slide-by-slide narrative
- speaker-note drafts
- Q&A preparation
- title and abstract options
- nontechnical summary

## What The Scholar Must Verify

- claims about results
- identification language
- economic magnitude
- interpretation of figures
- audience fit
- timing

## Common Mistakes

- making slides too text-heavy
- hiding the research question
- skipping identification
- using generic motivation
- overstating external validity

## Disclosure Notes

Disclosure is usually not needed for ordinary presentation drafting, but record substantial AI assistance in your internal project log.
