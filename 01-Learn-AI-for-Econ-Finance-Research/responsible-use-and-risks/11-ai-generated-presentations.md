# AI-Generated Presentations

## What Problem This Solves

AI-generated slides can look polished while changing the meaning of results, figures, or claims.

## Risks

- AI may simplify results incorrectly.
- AI may turn cautious claims into strong claims.
- AI may change chart labels, units, or legends.
- AI may create figures that look empirical but are only illustrative.
- AI may expose unpublished work if deployed publicly.
- Animation can distract from identification, mechanism, and evidence.
- Speaker notes may invent answers to likely questions.

## Economics And Finance Rules

- Never let AI rewrite causal identification claims without checking.
- Never let AI turn correlation into causality.
- Never let AI simplify standard errors, event-study plots, or robustness tables incorrectly.
- Never use illustrative financial charts where empirical charts are expected.
- Never deploy slides publicly before checking coauthor, data, and conference constraints.

## What The Scholar Must Verify

- all numbers
- all labels
- all causal language
- all figure sources
- all speaker-note answers
- privacy and sharing settings
