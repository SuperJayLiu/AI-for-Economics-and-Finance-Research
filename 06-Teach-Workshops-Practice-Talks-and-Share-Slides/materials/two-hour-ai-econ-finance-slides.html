0-5 minutes

The promise and the trap

AI can make research labor cheaper.
AI does not make output true.

The goal is not to produce more research-looking artifacts. The goal is to build accountable workflows.

Core rule

AI output is not evidence

Treat AI output as a draft, checklist, critique, explanation, or coding aid.

Evidence still comes from sources, data, code, equations, institutional facts, and human judgment.

What AI is good at

Use AI where the work is structured and checkable

Literature

Compare supplied papers, build matrices, flag missing facts.

Code

Explain errors, draft functions, build toy tests, inspect scripts.

Writing

Improve clarity, structure, flow, and audience fit.

Data Work

Plan pipelines, variable dictionaries, merge audits, EDA.

Presenting

Build talk outlines, speaker notes, and Q&A drills.

Management

Create checklists, logs, issues, project rules, and workflows.

What not to automate

Do not outsource scholarly judgment

Do not let AI decide

  • final research question
  • identification credibility
  • causal interpretation
  • literature contribution

Do not let AI handle blindly

  • confidential referee material
  • restricted data
  • final submission readiness
  • public policy or investment claims

Live demo 1

Casual prompt vs controlled workflow

Casual

Summarize this paper and tell me the contribution.

Controlled

Using only the text I provide, create a structured research note for an economics/finance audience. Return: question, data/setting, method/design/model, cautious main claim, unverifiable items, facts to check, and three seminar questions. Do not invent citations, results, datasets, robustness checks, or institutional details. If unclear, ask up to five questions.

105-115 minutes

Failure cases make the rules concrete

The point is not to distrust everything. The point is to match each AI output to the right verification method.

Failure case 1

Fake citation

Why it looks plausible

The title sounds field-appropriate. The journal name and year look normal.

How to catch it

Search title, DOI, journal page, author/year. Then check whether the source supports the exact sentence.

Failure case 2

Wrong code that runs

Code that runs can still be wrong.

Use toy data with a known answer before trusting AI-written merge, event-study, portfolio-sort, or table code.

Failure case 3

Slide title turns correlation into causality

Bad title

"Bank closures reduce small-firm employment."

Safer title

"Employment falls after closures in treated areas; causal interpretation depends on the design."
